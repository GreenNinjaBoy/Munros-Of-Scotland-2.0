What is a Munro?


The Munros are mountains in the country of Scotland with a height of over 3000 feet. You can 'bag' a munro by climbing it and recording it on a map. The offical list of these mountains comes to a total of 283.

Why are they called Munros?


Munros are named after Sir Hugh Munro, 4th Baronet (1856â€“1919), who produced the first list of such hills, known as Munro's Tables, in 1891. Sir Hugh also added what he considered lesser peaks now known today as 'Munro-Tops' which are also over 3000 feet but lower than the nearby primary mountain. As off December 2020 the total number of Munros are 282 with 226 Munro-tops.
